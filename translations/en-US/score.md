## Scoring Rules ##
1. An owned set with no hand type may still win, but scores 0 points.
2. Outside the General category, the same category does not count multiple times.
3. If the set can be split into multiple combinations, compute the score of each combination and take the highest-scoring combination's score as the score.
4. The score cap is 16 points; a total exceeding 16 points is counted as 16 points, and this cap is called the "score cap".

# Categories and Descriptions #

## General category ##

Each of these can stand as its own category, so they are combined into the General category.

#### Fully Concealed: 2 points

Win by draw, with no chow, pung, or open quartet (added quartet, claimed quartet) before the win.

#### All Claimed: 2 points

All three sets before the win came from chow, pung, or open quartet, and the win is by discard.

#### Value Honors: 2 points

The owned set has a triplet or quartet of one's own seat honor or the luminaries.

#### All Triplets: 3 points

The owned set has one pair and three triplets or quartets.

## Chance category ##

Independent of the owned set's content, these are bonus points awarded for a low-probability event.

#### Last Tile Draw: 2 points

Win by draw on the last live wall tile (the last tile in the wall other than the dead wall).

#### Last Tile Claim: 2 points

Win by discard on the last discard (the tile discarded by another player after the last live wall tile is drawn).

#### After a Kong: 2 points

Win by draw on the replacement tile (the tile drawn from the wall tail after a kong).

#### Robbing a Kong: 2 points

Win by discard via "Robbing a Kong" when another player calling an added quartet.

#### Heavenly Draw: 6 points

The dealer's 11 dealt tiles already form a winning set.

#### Earthly Draw: 6 points

A non-dealer wins by draw on their first draw, regardless of whether other players have claimed.

## Honors category ##

#### Two Luminaries: 2 points

The owned set has two triplets or quartets of luminaries.

#### Little Three Dragons: 4 points

The owned set has two triplets or quartets of dragons, plus one pair of seat honors.

#### Big Three Dragons: 16 points

The owned set has three triplets or quartets of dragons.

## Terminals category ##

The owned set is composed entirely of, or entirely without, terminal-or-honor-related tiles.

#### All Simples: 2 points

The owned set is composed entirely of simple tiles.

#### Common Ends: 1 point

Every meld and the pair in the owned set contains a terminal-or-honor tile.

#### Perfect Ends: 2 points

Every meld and the pair in the owned set contains a terminal tile.

#### Common Terminals: 4 points

The owned set is composed entirely of terminal-or-honor tiles.

#### Perfect Terminals: 12 points

The owned set is composed entirely of terminal tiles.

## Single-Suit category ##

The owned set is composed entirely of one suit, or of that suit mixed with honor tiles.

#### Common Flush: 3 points

The owned set is composed of number tiles of a single suit plus honor tiles.

#### Perfect Flush: 6 points

The owned set is composed entirely of number tiles of a single suit.

#### All Honors: 16 points

The owned set is composed entirely of honor tiles.

#### Six Gates: 16 points

A Perfect Flush in the six-sided waiting set "1112345666", winning on any number tile of the same suit.

## Three-Suit category ##

The owned set has melds of the same number across all three suits.

#### Mixed Sequences: 3 points

Three sequences of the same numbers across the three suits.

#### Mixed Triplets: 12 points

Three triplets or quartets of the same number across the three suits.

## Identical-Sequences category ##

The owned set has two or more completely identical sequences.

#### Doubled Sequence: 2 points

The owned set has two completely identical sequences.

#### Tripled Sequence: 12 points

The owned set has three completely identical sequences.

## Concealed-Triplets category ##

The owned set has multiple concealed triplets or concealed quartets. If a concealed triplet becomes a claimed quartet, it no longer counts as a concealed triplet.

#### Two Concealed Triplets: 1 point

The owned set has two concealed triplets or quartets.

#### Three Concealed Triplets: 3 points

The owned set has three concealed triplets or quartets.

## Consecutive-Triplets category ##

The owned set has triplets or quartets of consecutive numbers in the same suit.

#### Two Consecutive Triplets: 3 points

The owned set has two triplets or quartets of consecutive numbers in the same suit.

#### Three Consecutive Triplets: 6 points

The owned set has three triplets or quartets of consecutive numbers in the same suit.

## Quartets category ##

The owned set has quartets, each formed from four tiles of the same kind through a kong.

#### One Quartet: 2 points

The owned set has one quartet.

#### Two Quartets: 6 points

The owned set has two quartets.

#### Three Quartets: 16 points

The owned set has three quartets.
